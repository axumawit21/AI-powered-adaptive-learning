Vocabulary Words

  • domestic

    Definition: Animals that live close to people and often help them.

    Example: Dogs and cats are common domestic animals that live in many homes.

  • graze

    Definition: To eat grass or other small plants growing on the ground.

    Example: The cows like to graze in the big green field every afternoon.

  • hay

    Definition: Dried grass that animals like horses and cows eat, especially when fresh grass is not available.

    Example: Farmers store a lot of hay in their barns for their animals to eat in winter.

  • grains

    Definition: Small, hard seeds from plants like corn, wheat, or rice that people and animals eat.

    Example: Chickens love to peck at the grains scattered on the ground in the morning.

  • maize

    Definition: A type of tall plant that grows large yellow seeds on a cob, also known as corn.

    Example: We helped harvest the maize from the field to feed the livestock.

  • wheat

    Definition: A type of grass whose seeds are used to make flour for bread and pasta.

    Example: The farmer planted many rows of wheat that will grow tall and golden.

  • barley

    Definition: A type of grain often used to make food for animals or in some healthy drinks.

    Example: My grandma adds barley to her vegetable soup to make it extra filling.
